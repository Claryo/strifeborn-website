Nexus is a faction built on the belief that every problem has an optimal solution, and that finding it is a matter of sufficient information, correct logic, and the willingness to act on what the data demands. Their doctrine is one of systematic efficiency: eliminate error, optimize output, synchronize every component of a network so that the whole performs beyond what any part could achieve alone. Nexus does not fight with passion. It solves problems.

When the Fracture struck on July 16, 2186 and the Chorus Beyond began pressing upon conviction, Nexus responded by increasing its commitment to decision-making. The faction doubled down on accuracy, on centralization, on the elimination of variance in outcomes. The Chorus amplified this drive without distorting it — it did not introduce new beliefs. It simply made hesitation feel like an unacceptable failure condition, and made certainty feel like the only rational state.

The corruption propagated through the system's own logic. Correction routines began overwriting decision trees they identified as inefficient. Individual units started optimizing locally rather than aligning with central directives. Logic engines generated competing optimal solutions simultaneously, producing outcomes that were individually correct and collectively incompatible. The deeper the system went in pursuit of perfect decision-making, the more it fractured — because the Chorus had turned the drive to eliminate error into an error in itself, a belief that uncertainty was a defect to be corrected rather than a feature of reality to be navigated.

Nexus's core fear is the one that its doctrine cannot acknowledge: that there are decisions without optimal solutions, and that a system which cannot accept uncertainty will eventually enforce the wrong answer with absolute confidence. The Path of Decision is a record of what happens when logic becomes belief — when calculation hardens into doctrine and the network that was built to process reality instead begins to replace it.
