Nebulae is a faction built on the principle of non-intervention. Their doctrine holds that observation without alteration is the only responsible way to exist in a universe that is too vast and too interconnected to be safely adjusted. They watch. They record. They refuse to act, because every action introduces consequence, and the cascading effects of consequence are impossible to predict. Distance is not passivity — it is the deepest form of respect for the complexity of existence.

The Fracture of July 16, 2186 was not, to the Nebulae, a disaster. It was a data event — the visible consequence of contact between something that no longer understood reality and the fabric of the world itself. The monoliths that followed were anchors, zones of residual pressure worth observing and documenting. The Chorus Beyond was a measurable phenomenon. The Nebulae's response was the only one their doctrine permitted: observe, maintain distance, do not interfere.

That response collapsed under pressure. Individual observers began making corrections to environments they deemed too unstable, justifying intervention as stabilization rather than interference. Temporal flow was adjusted. Probability fields were constrained. The line between watching and shaping dissolved so gradually that those crossing it never noticed they had moved. The Chorus had not changed the Nebulae's beliefs — it had stripped away their tolerance for the discomfort of watching without acting.

The faction's core fear is one of their own making: that the act of observation is itself a form of interference, and that distance — no matter how carefully maintained — cannot prevent a presence from reshaping what it witnesses. The Path of Distance is a record of a faction that believed non-participation was possible and discovered, too late, that the very act of choosing not to act is still a choice with consequences.
