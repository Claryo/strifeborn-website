Eclipse is a faction built on the belief that survival is not a right — it is earned through the hunt. Their doctrine centers on the cycle: death feeds life, weakness is culled so strength may persist, and the jungle answers those who prove themselves worthy of its favor. The hunt is not cruelty. It is the mechanism by which the world sustains itself, and Eclipse exists to embody and honor that mechanism.

When the Fracture struck on July 16, 2186 and the Chorus Beyond began pressing upon conviction, Eclipse responded by unleashing instinct. The path of the hunt intensified. Rituals grew more frequent, more violent, and less restrained. The Chorus amplified what the faction already believed — that hesitation was weakness, that survival demanded immediacy, that the cycle of blood was not merely natural but sacred and absolute.

The corruption did not arrive as rebellion. It came as refinement. Hunters began experimenting with forbidden tools — scavenged technology, biomechanical augments, synthetic enhancements — each justified as a way to become more effective. The cycle was not abandoned; it was optimized. What had once been a living ritual became an engineering project. The bond between hunter and beast, between Eclipse and its jungle, was not broken — it was replaced with something assembled, something fabricated, something that did not breathe.

Eclipse's core fear is extinction through excess — the moment the hunt continues without the cycle that gives it meaning. When killing becomes the only function rather than one part of a larger balance, the faction does not grow stronger. It hollows out. The Path of the Hunt is a record of what happens when the instinct to survive devours the very thing worth surviving for.
