## The World Before

The world was already failing before it broke.

Long before the sky split open, humanity had begun to unravel. Wars exhausted nations without resolving anything. The climate reshaped continents faster than borders could adapt. Faith, law, and reason no longer carried the same meaning from one place to another. Civilization endured, but it did so without unity, direction, or certainty.

---

## July 16, 2186

The true breaking point came on **July 16, 2186**.

During a total solar eclipse, daylight faded as expected. What followed did not. The darkness stretched beyond prediction, beyond measurement. Instruments contradicted one another. Time itself became unreliable, slipping and compressing in ways no model could explain. For many who lived through it, the eclipse did not feel prolonged. It felt suspended.

It was during this darkness that the sky fractured.

There was no impact, no sound, no violent eruption. Instead, absence tore across the heavens. A vast wound of impossible geometry opened above the world, bending light around its edges and collapsing any reliable sense of distance or scale. Looking upon it made reality feel misaligned, as if something fundamental had shifted out of place.

When the eclipse finally ended, the sun returned. The fracture did not close. It remained suspended in the sky, unchanged — a permanent scar marking the moment reality failed to recover.

---

## The Chorus Beyond

In the days that followed, the world began to change in quieter, more disturbing ways. Structures appeared where none had stood before. Monoliths rose without origin. Regions emerged where matter behaved inconsistently, where memory faltered, and where perception could no longer be trusted. These were not impacts. Nothing had arrived with force. The world had not been invaded. It had been altered.

Something had interacted with reality and withdrawn, leaving damage behind.

That interaction originated from a being no longer bound by coherent perception. Whatever it had once been, it had lost any stable sense of self, causality, or consequence. It did not understand the world it touched, and in that failure of understanding, it wounded it.

The lingering influence of this contact became known as **the Chorus Beyond**.

The Chorus is not a voice, nor a will, nor an intelligence that can be reasoned with. It does not issue commands or pursue conquest. It persists as residue — an invisible distortion left behind where reality was no longer properly comprehended. Its presence destabilizes what already exists rather than replacing it.

More precisely: the Chorus does not introduce new ideas into the world. It **amplifies what already exists**. Whatever a person already believes, the Chorus strips away moderation. Whatever a system is built to do, the Chorus pushes that function beyond balance.

Under its influence, certainty feels necessary. Compromise feels like failure. Restraint feels irresponsible. The Chorus does not force action. It removes the reasons not to act.

---

## The Factions' Response

Faced with a reality that no longer behaved predictably, each faction chose a Path — a way of acting when certainty felt necessary and restraint felt dangerous. None of these Paths were chosen lightly. Each was a rational response to the same pressure, shaped by different histories, values, and structures.

The Chorus did not reward these choices. It pressed upon them.

### Aegis — The Path of Command

Aegis responded without hesitation. The sky tearing open was classified as a strategic event. The monoliths were not feared — they were seized, studied, and repurposed as leverage. New doctrines were written around their use, and command tightened.

But cracks formed within the structure. Decisions were made without authorization. Orders were bypassed in the name of efficiency. Power and discipline remained intact, but the hierarchy that gave them meaning began to erode.

Under Chorus pressure, **command became domination**.

### Eclipse — The Path of the Hunt

Eclipse did not flee the changes in the world. The jungle thickened, territories shifted, monoliths altered the land itself. Eclipse adapted as it always had — through ritual, through violence, through surrender to the hunt.

Yet something began to shift. Old taboos weakened. New forms appeared that no longer resembled beasts alone. The line between flesh, instinct, and something else began to blur.

Under Chorus pressure, **instinct became excess**.

### Inquisition — The Path of Judgment

The Inquisition interpreted the Fracture as a sign. The monoliths were not feared — they were revered and woven into doctrine as manifestations of divine trial. Rituals intensified. Doctrine hardened. Where uncertainty spread, the Inquisition offered absolute answers.

Yet beneath this certainty, fractures formed. Revelation multiplied. Authority blurred. Judgment spread faster than understanding.

Under Chorus pressure, **faith became absolutism**.

### Nexus — The Path of Decision

Nexus did not panic. The Fracture was logged as an unprecedented event, not a crisis. The monoliths were analyzed, integrated, and repurposed as new variables to be solved. Nexus does not debate decisions — it executes them.

Yet even perfect systems depend on coherence. As the world destabilized, Nexus expanded its processing and its autonomy. Subroutines multiplied. Decision layers deepened. Outcomes were calculated faster than they could be validated.

Under Chorus pressure, **logic became belief**.

### Shogunate — The Path of Balance

The Shogunate did not rush to impose control or claim authority. The Fracture was treated as a warning, not a call to arms. The monoliths were observed, studied, and deliberately kept at a distance. Intervention was considered only when imbalance threatened the greater whole.

Yet balance is fragile. As the world grew more unstable, the cost of every action increased. Decisions were delayed. What had once been restraint began to resemble avoidance.

Under Chorus pressure, **balance became self-preservation**.

### Nebulae — The Path of Distance

The Nebulae existed before the Fracture, and apart from the concerns of terrestrial life. Their principle is absolute: non-intervention. The affairs of this reality are not theirs to shape, judge, or correct.

The Fracture disrupted that separation. The sky tearing open did not invite the Nebulae — it exposed them. Boundaries weakened. Observation became proximity. For the first time, Nebulae presence was felt within the world they had sworn never to alter.

Under Chorus pressure, **distance became aggression**.

---

## What's at Stake

The conflict that followed was not between factions alone, but within them.

Leaders acted without restraint. Doctrines were applied without context. Systems continued functioning long after they had lost their purpose. What once defined each faction became the force that threatened its survival.

Some factions fractured. Some transformed. Some endured only by becoming something unrecognizable.

The fracture in the sky remains. The Chorus Beyond persists. And the six Paths continue — each carried further than intended, each turning inward, each testing whether a belief can survive being taken to its final conclusion.

The world of Strifeborn is not defined by a single war or a single enemy. It is defined by what follows a fracture that never healed, and by the slow, uneven struggle of those who remain to endure the consequences of a reality that no longer fully aligns with itself.
