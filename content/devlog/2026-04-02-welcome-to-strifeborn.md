Hey, and welcome.

If you're reading this, you've found the development blog for **Strifeborn** — a real-time auto-battler I've been building solo. This first post is a brief introduction to the game and what this devlog will be about.

## What is Strifeborn?

Strifeborn is a tactical auto-battler where you architect a team of heroes before the fight begins, then watch them battle autonomously. No clicking, no reaction-based gameplay — just pure strategy. The question isn't "can you execute?" It's "did you build the right thing?"

The game features **six factions**, **42 heroes**, a deep synergy system (faction, class, and hero-specific synergies), a blessing system that shapes each run, and faction oaths that commit you to a playstyle from the start. There's a lot of interlocking systems, and that's intentional — the goal is depth through interaction, not complexity for its own sake.

## The Design Philosophy

The core idea is simple: **decisions before combat matter more than decisions during combat**. The tension lives in the preparation phase — choosing which heroes to recruit, which synergies to activate, which items to forge, which blessing to take. Once the round starts, you watch and learn.

This means every system has to be readable and honest. No hidden information. No RNG you can't account for. If your team lost, you should be able to look at the replay and understand exactly why.
