## The Chorus Beyond — Campaign Mode

**The Chorus Beyond** is the primary game mode of Strifeborn. It is a single-player, fixed-length PvE campaign that serves two purposes: it tells the story of the fractured world across six faction story arcs, and it is the definitive space for learning and mastering every core system the game has to offer.

Each faction has **5 Chapters**. Each Chapter wraps exactly one full Campaign run. Chapters are linear and non-branching — they tell one canonical story per faction, and completing Chapter N unlocks Chapter N+1.

---

## Run Structure

Every Campaign run follows the same strict, immutable structure.

### Acts and Waves

- **5 Acts** per run
- **5 Waves** per Act
- **25 Waves** total

Every Act follows the same internal wave pattern:

1. Normal Wave
2. Normal Wave
3. Swarm Wave
4. Normal Wave
5. Elite Wave

In **Act V**, the Elite Wave at Wave 25 is replaced by a **Boss Wave**.

### Wave Types

| Wave Type | Description | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Normal | Standard encounter testing baseline team strength and positioning | Standard Gold |
| Swarm | High enemy count, low individual strength — stresses AoE, sustain, and frontline stability | Blessing selection |
| Elite | High-difficulty encounter; enemies may have modifiers, items, or corruption | Shop access + bonus Gold |
| Boss | Major encounter at Wave 25, concludes the Chapter run | Chapter completion reward |

---

## Lives System

Each Campaign run starts with **5 lives**.

- Losing a wave consumes one life and the same wave is replayed
- Enemy composition and tier remain unchanged on retry
- A small compensatory reward is granted after a loss to help adjust your team
- The run ends immediately when you reach **0 lives**

Victory is achieved by clearing all 25 waves.

---

## Inter-Wave Systems

Certain wave types guarantee specific rewards between waves:

- **After every Swarm Wave** — you choose one of three **Blessings**
- **After every Elite Wave** — you gain access to the **Shop**

These guarantees create a predictable rhythm of pressure and choice. In a full 25-wave Campaign run, you will receive exactly **5 Blessings** and access the **Shop** after each Elite Wave.

---

## Deploy Slots

You start each run with a **deployment cap of 3 heroes**. The cap cannot be increased during the very first preparation phase.

Increasing deployment capacity costs Gold, with progressive costs. The default maximum is **12 deploy slots** (hard cap of 15 under special effects).

### Team Size Progression

You begin with an initial pool of 5 heroes. At specific waves, you are offered a roster of 5 hero options and choose 1 to add permanently:

- Wave 2
- Wave 4
- Wave 7
- Wave 9

This brings your maximum roster to **9 heroes** by the late game.

## Difficulty Tiers

The Campaign supports five difficulty levels, unlocked progressively. Completing Difficulty X unlocks Difficulty X+1; completing Chapter X unlocks Chapter X+1. These two progression tracks are independent.

| Tier | Flavor |
|---|---|
| **Dissonance** | The pressure is present, but unstable. Beliefs still waver. Most can still hesitate. |
| **Whisper** | The Chorus is faint, but constant. Doubt begins to feel dangerous. |
| **Fracture** | The pressure is undeniable. Conviction hardens. Every choice carries weight. |
| **Dominance** | The Chorus presses without pause. Belief no longer tolerates restraint. |
| **Ascendency** | The pressure is absolute. There is no hesitation, no retreat, no doubt. |

Difficulty affects enemy starting tier and stat scaling only. It never alters Chapter rules, boss mechanics, or the wave structure.

**Difficulty X means Wave 1 enemies start at Tier X.** Enemy tiers increase by +1 with each new Act.

## Overdrive

To prevent combat from stalling indefinitely, **Overdrive** activates 30 seconds after combat begins. It applies escalating global modifiers to both sides equally and cannot be prevented or removed.

| Stage | Damage Multiplier | Healing & Shielding |
|---|---|---|
| Overdrive I (30s) | ×2 | −33% |
| Overdrive II (35s) | ×3 | −66% |
| Overdrive III (40s) | ×4 | −100% |

Each stage lasts 5 seconds. Combat speed is doubled during Overdrive. Compositions that rely on sustained healing become progressively less viable as Overdrive escalates — build accordingly.

## Mana System Overview

Each hero has **Maximum Mana** (how much is needed to cast their Spell) and **Starting Mana** (how much they begin combat with, defined in increments of 0%, 25%, 50%, 75%, or 100%).

Mana is generated by:
- Dealing damage with Basic Attacks
- Taking damage
- Ability effects, items, and blessings

A Spell fires automatically the moment Mana reaches or exceeds Maximum Mana. Mana overflow is retained after casting (if you had 130 mana and cast at 100, you keep 30). Heroes with a **Passive** Spell always have Maximum Mana = 0 and cast continuously rather than through the mana system.

## Post-Chapter Encounter — The Chorus Beyond

After defeating the Chapter boss, you may optionally face **The Chorus Beyond** itself. This is not a combat challenge — it is a performance benchmark.

- The Chorus Beyond is invincible, does not attack, and does not interact with your team
- The encounter is time-limited
- Your objective is to deal as much damage as possible (total DPS output)
- No lives are consumed and completion is unaffected
- Results are ranked asynchronously against other players, with weekly resets and weekly rewards

This exists as a meta-performance lens, not as PvP competition.

---

## Preparation Phase

Campaign mode is single-player, meaning the preparation phase has **no time limit**. You may inspect enemies, adjust your team, equip items, and reposition heroes freely before pressing Start Combat.

Once combat begins, the battlefield is fully locked. No unit positions, items, artifacts, or synergies can be changed until combat ends.
