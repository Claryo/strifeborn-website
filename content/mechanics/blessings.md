Blessings are the primary layer of adaptive progression during a run. Each one permanently alters the trajectory of your run by reinforcing a build direction, enabling a new interaction, or locking you into a commitment. They cannot be removed, replaced, or undone once chosen.

Blessings are designed to **force commitment**, not to fix mistakes. A run lost after a risky Blessing is a valid outcome, not a balance error.

---

## When You Get Them

Blessings are offered **only after Swarm Waves** — the third wave of every Act. In a standard Campaign run (25 waves, 5 Acts), you will receive exactly **5 Blessings** per run.

At each Blessing event:
- You are offered **3 choices**, all of the same rarity
- You must pick exactly one
- Your default reroll count is **2 per Blessing screen**
- The chosen Blessing is removed from the run pool — it will never appear again in that run

---

## Rarity Tiers

All Blessings belong to one of three rarity tiers. The distribution shifts as the run progresses, making higher-rarity Blessings more likely in later Acts.

| Act | Bronze | Silver | Gold |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 65% | 30% | 5% |
| 2 | 55% | 35% | 10% |
| 3 | 45% | 40% | 15% |
| 4 | 35% | 45% | 20% |
| 5 | 25% | 50% | 25% |

### Bronze
Reliable, consistent, and low-overhead. Primarily flat stat bonuses or minor structural improvements. Bronze Blessings stabilize a direction without locking it.

### Silver
Modify how systems or hero kits behave. Introduce new interactions and require anticipation. Silver Blessings begin to shape a build.

### Gold
Define playstyle. Introduce permanent rules or scaling vectors. Lock the run into a direction. Gold Blessings are powerful, polarizing, and risky — they are a commitment, not an upgrade.

---

## Five Categories

Every Blessing belongs to exactly one category.

### Hero Blessings
Apply to a single specific hero and permanently modify their stats or kit behavior. These are the most personal and run-defining Blessings in the game. Hero Blessings are defined per hero and live in each hero's kit entry.

- **Bronze:** flat stat increases, small survivability boosts, consistency improvements
- **Silver:** modify one component of the kit (Basic Attack, Spell, or Passive)
- **Gold:** permanent scaling tied to hero actions, structural role changes

### Synergy Blessings
Affect Faction, Class, or Extra synergies through tokens, artifacts, and counter increases. These shape composition direction without bypassing synergy rules. The synergy chosen is randomly selected from those relevant to your current hero pool, with a small chance of being outside it.

- **Bronze:** gain +1 Synergy Token toward a specific synergy
- **Silver:** gain 1 Synergy Artifact (equippable, grants +1 synergy counter)
- **Gold:** gain 1 Synergy Artifact plus an additional effect or Advanced Item

### Economy Blessings
Modify Gold income, interest, shop rules, or refund rates. Fully deterministic and pre-combat only. Economy Blessings trade immediate power for long-term leverage.

Notable examples:
- **Bronze — Startup Fund:** Gain 3 Gold immediately; +3 Gold at the start of each Act
- **Silver — Loan Shark:** Gain 20 Gold now; lose 2 Gold per wave for the next 5 waves
- **Silver — Black Market:** Shop Level +1; each shop offers 1 additional guaranteed Advanced Item slot
- **Gold — Speculator:** After each wave, gain Gold equal to 20% of your current Gold (max +10/wave)
- **Gold — Chorus Patronage:** Whenever you select a Blessing, gain +5 Gold

### Player Blessings
Affect preparation and progression — recruiting, items, deployment, and meta-resources. Player Blessings reshape how the run is managed, not how fights are executed.

Notable examples:
- **Bronze — Tactical Expansion:** +1 Deploy Slot
- **Bronze — Vanguard I:** Frontline heroes that survive each wave permanently gain +2% Max HP
- **Silver — Adaptive Strategy:** Blessing screens offer 1 additional choice for the run
- **Silver — Reserve Corps:** Heroes on the bench gain +1% to all stats per wave (applies on deployment)
- **Gold — Fate's Gambit:** Double all Gold gains. Immediately lose 2 Player Lives.
- **Gold — Final Stand:** The first time you would reach 0 lives, survive at 1. All allies then gain +35% Damage Amp and +25% Attack Speed for the rest of the run.

### Teamwide Blessings
Apply global combat rules to all allies symmetrically. These define how your entire team functions as a system during combat.

Notable examples:
- **Bronze — Battle Cry:** All allies gain +20% Strength, +20% Wisdom, and +20% Attack Speed for the first 5 seconds of battle
- **Bronze — Hardened Armor:** All allies gain +40 Physical Defense and +40 Spell Defense
- **Silver — Pack Hunters:** All allies deal +3% bonus damage for each other ally currently alive
- **Silver — Critical Mass:** All allies gain +8% Crit Chance and +20% Crit Damage
- **Gold — Glass Cannon:** All allies gain +35% Strength and +40% Crit Damage. All allies lose −20% Max HP.
- **Gold — Synchrony:** All active synergies count as having +1 additional unit toward their thresholds. All allies start battle with −15% Strength and −15% Wisdom.
- **Gold — Ascension:** All allies gain +1% Strength, +1% Wisdom, and +1% Max HP per second during battle (resets each battle). All allies start at 50% Max HP.

## Rules and Constraints

- All Blessing effects are **fully deterministic** — no hidden randomness in resolution
- Blessings are **permanent** for the duration of the run
- **Conflicting Blessings are allowed** — resolving conflicts is your responsibility
- Blessings never scale with wave number, enemy tier, time in combat, or Overdrive
- Only Hero Blessings may introduce explicit, bounded scaling
- Blessings do not modify Overdrive timing or behavior in any way
- Blessing effects are not directly visualized on units but are fully inspectable through the Blessings interface
