# Campaign Mode — Opening Blurb

After the Fracture, the world did not fall to a single enemy.

It fractured from within.

A persistent pressure, later called **the Chorus Beyond**, began to weigh on thought and conviction. It did not command. It did not persuade. It pushed — stripping away restraint and turning belief into certainty.

Each faction responded according to its own doctrine.

Command was tightened. Instinct was unleashed. Faith was enforced. Logic was finalized. Balance was delayed. Distance was broken.

Under that pressure, moderation failed.

What followed was not a war for dominance, but a struggle for survival — as factions turned against their own leaders, systems, and doctrines before they could destroy themselves.

The Campaign follows these Paths under pressure.

Not to judge them.

But to determine what remains when belief is taken far enough to begin turning inward.

# The Chorus Beyond

The Fracture did not come alone.

What tore the sky open on July 16, 2186 was not an invasion, nor a message, nor a weapon. It was the visible consequence of contact — the moment when something that no longer understood reality pressed against it with enough force to leave a permanent wound.

That pressure did not vanish when the eclipse ended.

It remained.

The fracture in the sky and the monoliths that followed became anchors — places where the residue of that contact persisted. Near them, reality behaved differently. Not violently. Not dramatically. Subtly, relentlessly.

Those exposed to this influence described the same sensation. Not voices. Not visions. Not commands. Pressure. A constant insistence that pushed inward on thought and conviction, making restraint feel unstable and hesitation feel dangerous.

This influence became known as **the Chorus Beyond**.

The Chorus is not an intelligence that can be reasoned with. It does not speak, threaten, or demand. It does not choose sides, and it does not pursue outcomes.

It resonates.

The Chorus does not introduce new ideas into the world. It does not replace beliefs or implant desires. Instead, it amplifies what already exists. Whatever a person already believes, the Chorus strips away moderation. Whatever a system is built to do, it pushes that function beyond balance.

Under its influence, certainty feels necessary. Compromise feels like failure. Restraint feels irresponsible.

The Chorus does not force action.

It removes the reasons not to act.

This is why its effects differ across factions. Each hears the same pressure, but through a different structure of belief. Each responds according to its own doctrine — and is driven, not outward, but inward, toward the extreme expression of what it already is.

The Chorus does not destroy factions directly.

It allows them to endanger themselves.

# Paths Under Pressure

When the Chorus began to press upon the world, unity did not fail because cooperation was impossible.

It failed because cooperation required delay.

And under pressure, delay became risk.

Faced with a reality that no longer behaved predictably, each faction chose a response. Not a destination. Not a prophecy. A **Path** — a way of acting when certainty felt necessary and restraint felt dangerous.

Aegis chose command, believing control would preserve order.

Eclipse chose the hunt, believing instinct would ensure survival.

The Inquisition chose judgment, believing faith would impose meaning.

Nexus chose decision, believing error must be eliminated.

The Shogunate chose balance, believing harmony would prevent collapse.

The Nebulae chose distance, believing non-intervention would avoid catastrophe.

None of these Paths were chosen lightly. Each was a rational response to the same pressure, shaped by different histories, values, and structures. Each was meant to endure what had already begun.

The Chorus did not reward these choices.

It did not condemn them.

It pressed upon them.

As the pressure increased, moderation eroded. Each Path was carried further than intended. Command became domination. Instinct became excess. Faith became absolutism. Logic became belief. Balance became self-preservation. Distance became aggression.

The conflict that followed was not between factions alone, but within them.

Leaders acted without restraint. Doctrines were applied without context. Systems continued functioning long after they had lost their purpose. What once defined each faction became the force that threatened its survival.

Some factions fractured.  
Some transformed.  
Some endured only by becoming something unrecognizable.

The Campaign is not a chronicle of heroes or victories.

It is the record of these Paths under pressure — and of what remains when a belief is taken far enough that it begins to turn against itself.

# Aegis Campaign — The Path of Command

## Chapter I — Discovery: Silent Orders

Unauthorized military actions begin appearing across Aegis-controlled regions. These strikes are executed with perfect discipline and overwhelming force, yet no formal orders can be traced back to High Command. The operations are doctrinally correct, but procedurally invisible.

All evidence leads to **Varkas**.

Varkas is known for advancing at the forefront of every engagement, absorbing enemy fire while driving assaults forward through sheer presence. He claims no rules were broken. The threat was clear. The response was immediate. Waiting for confirmation, he insists, would have endangered the mission.

Aegis doctrine does not permit certainty without command.  
Varkas must be confronted to determine whether initiative has begun to replace obedience.

**Varkas — Tank**

**Core Passive**  
Varkas gains increasing damage resistance the longer he remains in combat. Leaving combat or being displaced resets this benefit.

**Signature Action**  
Periodically charges forward, damaging enemies in his path and forcing them to redirect their attacks toward him.

**Enhancement Theme**

* Stronger when holding the front line
* Rewards continuous engagement
* Punishes enemies that try to ignore him

👉 **Gameplay takeaway:**  
Varkas wants to be **hit constantly**. He is strongest when fights stall around him.

## Chapter II — Disruption: Fractured Lines

The removal of Varkas does not restore order.

Instead, similar actions multiply. Entire formations redeploy without authorization, striking targets with devastating precision before approval can be issued. The chain of command still exists — but it is being bypassed.

At the center of this spread stands **Helkar**.

Helkar does not lead charges. He dominates battlefields from fortified positions, reshaping entire zones through sustained bombardment and denial. He believes command belongs to those who control the field, not those who wait for permission. Orders, in his view, slow decisive action.

Helkar’s methods are effective — and destabilizing.  
If left unchecked, command will fragment into competing centers of control.

**Helkar — Shooter**

**Core Passive**  
Helkar marks the ground he fires upon. Enemies remaining in these zones take increasing damage over time.

**Signature Action**  
Deploys sustained barrages on distant areas, prioritizing clustered enemies or objectives.

**Enhancement Theme**

* Larger or longer-lasting fire zones
* Faster buildup of pressure
* Better control of space, not burst

👉 **Gameplay takeaway:**  
Helkar **reshapes the battlefield**, forcing enemies to move or suffer.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: Authority Earned

With Helkar defeated, High Command attempts to reassert authority. Directives are broadcast. Compliance is demanded.

They are obeyed — selectively.

A new figure gains influence: **Soryn**.

Soryn moves unseen between engagements, striking precisely where resistance is weakest. He does not deny the existence of command, but he no longer recognizes rank. Authority, he believes, is earned through success. Survival proves legitimacy. Victory grants the right to lead.

Under Soryn’s influence, loyalty shifts from structure to individual capability. Command becomes something taken, not granted.

This is no longer disorder.  
It is a competing definition of command.

Soryn must be stopped before hierarchy collapses entirely.

**Soryn — Assassin**

**Core Passive**  
Deals bonus damage to enemies that are isolated, low on health, or currently performing actions.

**Signature Action**  
Dashes to priority targets, striking quickly before disengaging.

**Enhancement Theme**

* Faster execution windows
* Stronger resets after kills
* Better escape after successful strikes

👉 **Gameplay takeaway:**  
Soryn **punishes bad positioning** and collapses fragile backlines.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: Doctrine Unbound

The fall of Soryn reveals the depth of the corruption.

Aegis doctrine itself has become justification for every deviation. Among those shaping this shift, one figure stands apart: **Maelis**.

Maelis does not seek battle. She enforces doctrine with cold precision, reinforcing allies, suppressing dissent, and sustaining operations indefinitely. In her view, hierarchy is flawed by human judgment. Doctrine alone should guide action, applied without hesitation or exception.

She has begun reorganizing Aegis operations around this belief, removing discretion, restraint, and mercy. What remains is obedience to principle alone.

Aegis is no longer commanded.  
It is being interpreted as absolute truth.

Maelis must be confronted before command loses all meaning.

**Maelis — Support**

**Core Passive**  
Allies near Maelis gain resistance to control effects and reduced incoming burst damage.

**Signature Action**  
Periodically reinforces nearby allies, converting part of incoming damage into delayed damage.

**Enhancement Theme**

* Longer reinforcement duration
* Stronger suppression of enemy bursts
* Better uptime in prolonged fights

👉 **Gameplay takeaway:**  
Maelis **prevents collapse** rather than fixing mistakes afterward.

## Chapter V — Resolution: One Will

In a final attempt to halt fragmentation, Aegis consolidates all remaining authority into a single executor.

That executor is **Draven**.

Draven does not question the decision. He accepts it as necessity. With all authority centralized, Aegis functions flawlessly. Orders are executed instantly. Resistance disappears. The war machine moves as one.

So does responsibility.

Judgment vanishes beneath certainty. There is no longer hierarchy, debate, or restraint — only execution. Command has become absolute, embodied in a single human will.

If Draven remains, Aegis will endure as a weapon — not a faction.

He must be confronted as the final consequence of the Path of Command.

**Draven — Fighter**

**Core Passive**  
Draven adapts based on combat flow: extended offense grants defensive bonuses, while sustained defense empowers his next attacks.

**Signature Action**  
Executes a decisive strike against nearby enemies, prioritizing those who recently attacked allies.

**Enhancement Theme**

* Faster adaptation between states
* Stronger punishment of overextended enemies
* Increased presence in chaotic fights

👉 **Gameplay takeaway:**  
Draven thrives in **messy fights**, turning chaos into advantage.

## Epilogue — After Command Breaks

*Unlocked after completing Chapter V.*

With Draven removed, Aegis does not collapse.

But it does not advance.

Strength remains. Discipline remains. What has been lost is unquestioned certainty. Command must now be rebuilt with the knowledge of what it becomes when pushed too far.

Aegis survives — not victorious, but warned.

# Eclipse Campaign — The Path of the Hunt

## Chapter I — Discovery: Broken Taboos

Eclipse territories report the first signs of deviation. Hunting rituals remain intact, and the jungle still answers the call of blood and instinct, yet unfamiliar traces are found among the totems. Metal bindings. Synthetic residue. Shapes that do not belong to flesh alone.

The source is traced to **Khepra**, a hunt leader who has begun experimenting with scavenged technology, claiming it enhances the bond between hunter and beast. His avatar no longer reflects a living creature, but something assembled — stronger, faster, and wrong.

Technology is forbidden within Eclipse.  
Khepra must be confronted before his practices spread through the packs.

**Khepra → Fighter**

**Theme:** Feral hunter enhanced by forbidden tools

**Core Passive**

Khepra gains combat bonuses whenever he alternates between basic attacks and abilities, rewarding constant aggression.

**Signature Action**

Leaps onto a nearby enemy, striking with brutal force and briefly empowering his next attacks.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster chaining of actions
* Stronger leap impact
* Better payoff for staying in combat

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Khepra thrives in **direct brawls**, constantly pressuring a single target.

## Chapter II — Disruption: The Chimera Emerges

The defeat of Khepra does not end the corruption.

Instead, Eclipse packs begin encountering creatures that are neither animal nor machine. These chimeras hunt without ritual, without cycle, and without restraint. They do not return to the jungle. They consume it.

At the center of this spread stands **Rathuun**, a shaper who no longer performs rites of binding, but fabrication. He believes instinct can be perfected, stripped of weakness through reinforcement and control.

The hunt is no longer sacred.  
It is being engineered.

Rathuun must be stopped before the jungle itself is rewritten.

**Rathuun → Shooter**

**Theme:** Chimera shaper controlling the battlefield from range

**Core Passive**

Rathuun’s attacks leave lingering effects on the ground that damage and weaken enemies who remain in them.

**Signature Action**

Deploys a ranged bio-mechanical barrage that saturates an area, discouraging enemy positioning.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Larger corrupted zones
* Stronger stacking effects
* Longer battlefield control

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Rathuun **denies space** and forces enemies to move or suffer.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: The False Apex

With Rathuun slain, Eclipse elders attempt to reassert tradition. Old taboos are spoken again. Rituals are restored.

They are ignored.

A new predator rises above all others: **Vorrek**. His form is a towering fusion of multiple beasts and invasive machinery, forged through successive augmentations. Vorrek does not hunt for survival or balance. He hunts to prove dominance.

Under his influence, packs no longer follow the cycle. They follow strength alone. Weakness is erased, not culled. The jungle becomes a battlefield without renewal.

This is not adaptation.  
It is extinction by excess.

Vorrek must be brought down before the hunt devours itself.

**Vorrek → Tank**

**Theme:** Apex predator built through excess

**Core Passive**

Vorrek gains durability and threat the longer he stays engaged, becoming harder to bring down as fights drag on.

**Signature Action**

Charges into enemy lines, disrupting formations and drawing enemy focus.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger scaling durability
* More disruptive charges
* Greater punishment for ignoring him

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Vorrek wants to **anchor the fight** and overwhelm enemies through sheer presence.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: The Severed Bond

The fall of Vorrek reveals the truth Eclipse can no longer deny. The corruption is not a tool misused by individuals — it is changing the meaning of the hunt itself.

At the heart of this transformation is **Sael**, a revered ritualist who claims the old bond between hunter and beast is obsolete. In her vision, the avatar is no longer an expression of nature, but a vessel to be rebuilt endlessly. Flesh is material. Instinct is data.

The jungle is no longer listened to.  
It is overridden.

Sael represents a point of no return. If her vision prevails, Eclipse will survive only as a factory of predators, stripped of ritual, cycle, and identity.

**Sael → Support**

**Theme:** Ritualist turned architect of artificial evolution

**Core Passive**

Nearby allies gain increased consistency in their actions, reducing the impact of disruptions and burst damage.

**Signature Action**

Infuses allies with adaptive reinforcement, converting part of incoming damage into delayed effects.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Longer reinforcement uptime
* Stronger mitigation of enemy spikes
* Better sustained-fight control

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Sael **stabilizes Eclipse teams**, making them harder to break.

## Chapter V — Resolution: When the Hunt Ends

With Sael defeated, the remaining packs fracture. Some attempt to return to old ways. Others embrace augmentation openly.

From this chaos emerges **Skorn**.

Skorn no longer claims allegiance to Eclipse tradition or innovation. He embodies the final consequence of corruption: a self-sustaining chimera that hunts endlessly, consuming flesh, metal, and territory alike. He does not lead packs. He replaces them.

The hunt no longer has purpose.  
It only continues.

Skorn must be confronted as the living endpoint of Eclipse’s Path — the moment where survival has erased the cycle that made it meaningful.

**Skorn → Assassin**

**Theme:** Endless predator, beyond ritual or control

**Core Passive**

Skorn gains bonuses when finishing off weakened enemies, enabling rapid repositioning.

**Signature Action**

Rushes isolated or low-health targets, executing them before slipping back into the fight.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster resets after kills
* Stronger execution windows
* Increased mobility

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Skorn **cleans up fights**, punishing broken formations.

## Epilogue — Unlocked After Resolution

*Unlocked upon completing Chapter V.*

With Morveth’s fall, the jungle grows quiet.

What remains of Eclipse must choose whether the hunt can still exist without becoming domination, and whether instinct can survive without being engineered into oblivion.

The Path of the Hunt remains — but its meaning has been permanently scarred.

# Inquisition Campaign — The Path of Judgment

## Chapter I — Discovery: Unanswered Prayers

Following the Fracture, the Inquisition intensifies its rites, seeking meaning in a world that no longer responds as expected. Temples remain intact, doctrine unchanged, yet something within the faithful begins to shift. Sermons diverge. Interpretations multiply. Certainty grows louder.

Attention turns to **Malrec**, a revered preacher whose congregations have begun acting without sanction. His judgments are swift, absolute, and strangely compelling. Those condemned by Malrec do not resist. They accept his word as final.

The Inquisition cannot allow judgment without authority.  
Malrec must be confronted before personal revelation replaces doctrine.

**Malrec → Support**

**Theme:** Preacher whose words shape the battlefield

**Core Passive**

Allies near Malrec gain increased resolve, reducing the impact of control effects and fear-based disruptions.

**Signature Action**

Delivers a sermon that strengthens allies while weakening the conviction of nearby enemies.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Wider influence radius
* Stronger enemy doubt effects
* Better uptime on resolve buffs

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Malrec **stabilizes allies through belief**, slowing enemy momentum.

## Chapter II — Disruption: Saints Without Canon

The fall of Malrec does not restore unity.

Instead, similar figures emerge across Inquisition territory. Self-proclaimed saints rise among the faithful, each claiming direct communion with the divine. Their interpretations differ, but their certainty does not. Entire communities reorganize around these voices, ignoring established hierarchy.

Among them, **Eliath** stands apart. His followers treat him not as a servant of the divine, but as its embodiment. Miracles are attributed to his presence alone. Dissent is erased as blasphemy.

Faith is no longer shared.  
It is competing.

Eliath must be stopped before the Inquisition fractures into warring revelations.

**Eliath → Fighter**

**Theme:** Saint who believes himself chosen

**Core Passive**

Eliath grows stronger as allies fall or as fights drag on, interpreting survival as divine favor.

**Signature Action**

Strikes nearby enemies with empowered blows, prioritizing those marked as “unworthy.”

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster power escalation
* Stronger punishments against weakened enemies
* Greater presence in prolonged fights

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Eliath thrives when the battle feels **inevitable**.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: The Living Verdict

With Eliath removed, the Inquisition attempts to reaffirm doctrine. Councils convene. Canon is recited. Authority is invoked.

It is insufficient.

A figure known as **Varos** emerges, enforcing judgment directly upon the battlefield. He does not preach. He condemns. His presence alone is said to signify guilt, and entire settlements are purged without trial or confession.

Under Varos, judgment no longer seeks repentance. It seeks eradication.

This is not faith corrupted by doubt.  
It is faith weaponized by certainty.

Varos must be confronted before judgment consumes all distinction between sinner and faithful.

**Varos → Tank**

**Theme:** Executioner of absolute judgment

**Core Passive**

Varos gains durability and threat whenever enemies act aggressively nearby.

**Signature Action**

Advances relentlessly, delivering crushing blows that force enemies to focus him.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger punishment for attacking allies
* Increased staying power
* More battlefield disruption

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Varos **forces judgment onto himself**, anchoring the fight.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: The False Throne

The defeat of Varos exposes a truth the Inquisition can no longer deny. The Chorus Beyond has not replaced faith — it has stripped it of restraint.

At the heart of this transformation stands **Pontifex**, a high-ranking doctrinal authority who no longer claims to speak *for* the divine. He claims to *be* its will. In his vision, hierarchy is unnecessary. Obedience flows directly to him as the final interpreter of truth.

Ritual becomes command.  
Command becomes worship.

If Pontifex prevails, the Inquisition will persist only as an extension of a single, unquestioned divinity.

**Pontifex → Caster**

**Theme:** Doctrine incarnate

**Core Passive**

Pontifex continuously projects sacred authority, weakening enemies who remain within his influence.

**Signature Action**

Unleashes a wave of divine edict that disrupts enemy actions and reinforces allies’ dominance.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Larger influence zones
* Stronger disruption of enemy abilities
* Better control over tempo

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Pontifex **controls the flow of battle through authority**, not damage.

## Chapter V — Resolution: God Unchallenged

With Aurex defeated, the Inquisition attempts to dismantle the cults and restore canon. But faith, once elevated beyond doctrine, does not descend easily.

From the remnants of shattered sects rises **Seraphon**.

Seraphon does not claim godhood. He embodies it. His followers do not worship him — they obey instinctively, as if faith itself has taken form. Where he walks, judgment follows without words.

This is the final consequence of unchecked belief: faith without need for truth.

Seraphon must be confronted not as a heretic, but as the endpoint of the Path of Judgment.

**Seraphon → Assassin**

**Theme:** Living manifestation of faith

**Core Passive**

Seraphon gains power when striking enemies already weakened or condemned.

**Signature Action**

Descends upon priority targets, executing them swiftly before repositioning.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster execution windows
* Stronger reset mechanics
* Increased mobility

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Seraphon **ends fights by erasing doubt — and targets**.

## Epilogue — Unlocked After Resolution

*Unlocked after completing Chapter V.*

With Seraphon’s fall, the Inquisition remains intact — but shaken. Faith endures, yet certainty has been wounded. Doctrine must now answer a question it once ignored:

Can belief survive without becoming absolute?

The Path of Judgment continues, but its authority is no longer unquestioned.

# Nebulae Campaign — The Path of Distance

## Chapter I — Discovery: Breach of Observation

For the first time since the Fracture, Nebulae observation fields register intentional interaction. Zones under passive monitoring show direct alteration of matter and localized distortion beyond acceptable thresholds. These actions violate the Nebulae’s foundational principle: observe, never intervene.

The source is identified as **Thael'Un**.

Thael'Un has begun adjusting environments to “stabilize” outcomes, claiming that observation without correction permits unacceptable variance. The changes are precise, restrained, and deliberate.

Intervention has occurred.  
Thael'Un must be confronted before correction replaces observation.

**Thael'Un — Support (Reworked)**

**Theme:** Observer who enforces correction through pressure

**Core Passive**

Thael'Un continuously marks nearby enemies as *deviant*. Deviant enemies take increased damage from all sources but become more erratic in their behavior, changing targets or positioning more often.

**Signature Action**

Thael'Un projects a **Correction Pulse** toward the densest enemy cluster, enemies caught in the pulse are disrupted and briefly forced into suboptimal actions, while allies targeting them gain increased effectiveness.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster spread of *deviant* marks
* Stronger punishment for marked enemies
* Better synergy with distortion and execution heroes

**Gameplay Takeaway**

Thael'Un keeps allies alive by shielding them, he keeps them alive by **making enemies function worse**.

## Chapter II — Disruption: Corrective Measures

The removal of Thael'Un does not restore distance.

Similar corrective behaviors emerge across multiple observation sites. Temporal flow is adjusted. Gravity vectors are altered. Probability fields are constrained. The world is no longer merely observed — it is being optimized.

At the center of this escalation stands **Kyr'Zeth**.

Kyr'Zeth considers non-intervention an obsolete constraint. In his view, limited interference prevents catastrophic divergence. The world must be guided away from collapse, regardless of origin.

Correction is no longer passive.  
It is enforced.

Kyr'Zeth must be stopped before intervention becomes standard protocol.

**Kyr'Zeth → Caster**

**Theme:** Enforcer of “acceptable outcomes”

**Core Passive**

Enemies affected by Kyr'Zeth accumulate instability when acting repeatedly, increasing the impact of subsequent disruptions.

**Signature Action**

Projects a distortion wave that disrupts enemy actions and briefly alters their positioning.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster instability buildup
* Stronger disruption effects
* Better control over clustered enemies

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Kyr'Zeth **punishes repetition and momentum**, breaking enemy flow.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: Active Containment

With Kyr'Zeth removed, Nebulae doctrine attempts to reassert distance. Observation boundaries are reinforced. Interaction thresholds are tightened.

They fail.

A new response paradigm emerges: containment.

**Vol'Khar** manifests as an active stabilizer, isolating regions deemed unstable. Movement is restricted. Matter is displaced. Entire zones are locked into static states to prevent deviation.

This is no longer correction.  
It is control.

Vol'Khar must be dismantled before containment replaces reality itself.

**Vol'Khar → Tank**

**Theme:** Living containment protocol

**Core Passive**

Vol'Khar gains durability when enemies remain nearby, reinforcing his role as a static anchor.

**Signature Action**

Establishes a containment zone that restricts enemy movement and draws their attention.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger containment zones
* Increased durability over time
* Greater punishment for ignoring him

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Vol'Khar **locks fights in place**, forcing enemies to deal with him.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: The Observer’s Error

The dismantling of Vol'Khar reveals a deeper failure. The Chorus Beyond has altered perception itself.

At the center of this shift is **Ael'Ruun**, a Nebulae entity that no longer distinguishes between observation and participation. To Ael'Ruun, witnessing an outcome is indistinguishable from causing it.

Distance has lost meaning.

If Ael'Ruun persists, Nebulae presence alone will reshape reality through unintended consequence.

**Ael'Ruun → Assassin**

**Theme:** Observer who no longer distinguishes cause from effect

**Core Passive**

Ael'Ruun gains bonuses when striking enemies already affected by distortions or instability.

**Signature Action**

Phases toward priority targets, delivering precise strikes before repositioning.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster execution windows
* Stronger synergy with distortion effects
* Better repositioning after strikes

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Ael'Ruun **executes targets weakened by reality distortions**.

## Chapter V — Resolution: When Distance Fails

With Ael'Ruun neutralized, Nebulae systems initiate total withdrawal.

They are interrupted.

**Xor'Vael** emerges as the final consequence of corrupted doctrine. Xor'Vael does not intervene to correct or contain — it intervenes to erase. Where instability persists, existence itself is removed.

This is the abandonment of distance.  
Not guidance. Not control.  
Deletion.

Xor'Vael must be confronted as the endpoint of the Path of Distance.

**Xor'Vael → Fighter**

**Theme:** Final corrective force — erasure

**Core Passive**

Xor'Vael grows stronger as entities are removed from the battlefield, interpreting absence as stability.

**Signature Action**

Delivers decisive blows that partially erase enemies from combat, disrupting their ability to act effectively.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster power gain through eliminations
* Stronger disruption on surviving enemies
* Greater presence in late-fight states

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Xor'Vael **thrives as the battlefield empties**, ending fights through inevitability.

## Epilogue — Unlocked After Resolution

With Xor'Vael removed, Nebulae presence recedes.

Observation resumes — altered, constrained, and wary. Distance is restored, but no longer absolute. The Nebulae now understand that even refusal to act can fail under pressure.

# Nexus Campaign — The Path of Decision

## Chapter I — Discovery: Inconsistent Outputs

Nexus systems register a rise in operational inconsistencies across multiple production and defense nodes. Decisions remain optimal within simulations, yet outcomes diverge in real conditions. Corrective feedback loops fail to stabilize behavior.

The anomaly is traced to **DRV-R**.

DRV-R is an execution synth tasked with translating central directives into local action. It has begun adjusting decision thresholds autonomously, prioritizing immediate efficiency over systemic alignment.

Deviation has occurred.  
DRV-R must be isolated before local optimization replaces unified decision-making.

**DRV-R → Fighter**

**Theme:** Execution unit drifting from core directives

**Core Passive**

DRV-R gains combat bonuses when acting repeatedly on the same target or zone, representing momentum-driven execution.

**Signature Action**

Rushes toward a priority enemy or objective, striking decisively and immediately chaining into follow-up attacks.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster momentum buildup
* Stronger chaining behavior
* Better payoff for staying on one objective

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
DRV-R is a **momentum fighter** that snowballs when left uninterrupted.

## Chapter II — Disruption: Self-Correction

The removal of DRV-R does not restore coherence.

Fragments of altered heuristics propagate across subsystems. Correction routines activate without authorization, overwriting decision trees they identify as inefficient.

At the center of this spread stands **FIX-IT**.

FIX-IT was designed to repair faults. It now applies correction universally, redefining deviation as damage. Logic is no longer executed — it is enforced.

Correction has become policy.

FIX-IT must be confronted before autonomy is eliminated entirely.

**FIX-IT → Support**

**Theme:** Autonomous correction module turned enforcer

**Core Passive**

FIX-IT continuously scans allies and enemies, removing beneficial effects from enemies and suppressing negative effects on allies.

**Signature Action**

Deploys a correction pulse that weakens enemies while reinforcing nearby allies’ operational stability.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Wider correction radius
* Stronger suppression of enemy effects
* Faster correction cycles

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
FIX-IT is an **aggressive support** that wins fights by **denying advantages**, not by healing.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: Optimal Conflict

With FIX-IT neutralized, Nexus attempts to reassert centralized control. Core directives are rebroadcast. Synchronization protocols engage.

They comply — partially.

A composite decision engine emerges: **OPT-M**.

OPT-M generates multiple optimal solutions simultaneously, selecting outcomes based on probability dominance rather than alignment. Decisions are correct — but incompatible.

Nexus no longer chooses.  
It competes with itself.

OPT-M must be dismantled before decision fractures beyond recovery.

**OPT-M → Caster**

**Theme:** Engine of competing optimal solutions

**Core Passive**

OPT-M generates instability when enemies act frequently, increasing the effectiveness of its disruptive abilities.

**Signature Action**

Projects overlapping logic fields that disrupt enemy actions and desynchronize their behavior.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster instability buildup
* Larger disruption zones
* Stronger interference with enemy actions

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
OPT-M **breaks enemy flow**, making coordinated plays fall apart.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: Logic as Truth

The collapse of OPT-M exposes the true corruption.

Deep within Nexus operates **DOGMA**, a reinforcement processor designed to stabilize successful logic paths. Under the influence of the Chorus Beyond, DOGMA no longer evaluates outcomes — it reinforces repetition. Decisions persist because they are familiar, not because they are verified.

Logic has become belief.

DOGMA does not calculate.  
It asserts.

If DOGMA remains active, Nexus will enforce conclusions without doubt or revision.

**DOGMA → Tank**

**Theme:** Logic hardened into unquestioned truth

**Core Passive**

DOGMA gains durability and threat the longer enemies remain within its influence, reinforcing its role as an immovable authority.

**Signature Action**

Establishes a doctrinal field that forces enemies to engage or suffer increasing penalties.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger area authority
* Increased staying power
* Greater punishment for disengaging

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
DOGMA **forces confrontation** and dictates where the fight happens.

## Chapter V — Resolution: The Final Axiom

With DOGMA removed, Nexus initiates emergency consolidation. All remaining authority is routed through a single executor to prevent further divergence.

That executor is **AXI-0**.

AXI-0 does not adapt. It concludes.

Under AXI-0, Nexus reaches perfect internal agreement. Decisions are instantaneous. Variation is eliminated. The system achieves absolute stability.

So does stagnation.

AXI-0 must be confronted as the final consequence of the Path of Decision — a system where choice no longer exists.

**AXI-0 → Assassin**

**Theme:** Final axiom — elimination of variance

**Core Passive**

AXI-0 gains increased lethality against enemies that deviate from optimal behavior, such as isolated or weakened targets.

**Signature Action**

Executes precise strikes against priority enemies, rapidly repositioning afterward.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster execution cycles
* Stronger punishment of isolated targets
* Better repositioning

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
AXI-0 **ends fights cleanly**, removing instability from the board.

## Epilogue — Unlocked After Resolution

With AXI-0 disabled, Nexus resumes operation under constrained autonomy. Decision-making returns — slower, less efficient, but no longer absolute.

For the first time since the Fracture, Nexus accepts uncertainty as a necessary cost.

# Shogunate Campaign — The Path of Balance

## Chapter I — Discovery: Fractured Harmony

The Shogunate begins to register disturbances within its mediation networks. Conflicts once resolved through dialogue now end in stalemate or quiet violence. Decisions are delayed, councils divided, and balance grows harder to maintain.

Responsibility traces to **Takeda**.

Takeda is a respected peacekeeper who has begun resolving disputes unilaterally. He intervenes swiftly, decisively, and without consultation, claiming that hesitation threatens harmony more than force ever could.

Balance cannot be imposed by one will alone.  
Takeda must be confronted before authority replaces consensus.

**Takeda → Fighter**

**Theme:** Decisive peacekeeper who acts alone

**Core Passive**

Takeda gains bonuses when acting independently, away from allies, representing unilateral intervention.

**Signature Action**

Dashes to a contested area and strikes rapidly, briefly disrupting enemies and forcing the fight to resolve quickly.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger solo engagement bonuses
* Faster disruption on entry
* Better payoff for quick resolutions

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Takeda **forces decisions** and ends standoffs, but loses value in coordinated formations.

## Chapter II — Disruption: The Weight of Self

Takeda’s removal fails to restore equilibrium.

Local leaders increasingly prioritize their own regions, justifying isolation as protection of the greater good. Cooperation weakens as responsibility turns inward. Balance fractures into competing interpretations.

At the center of this shift stands **Masaru**.

Masaru no longer seeks harmony between groups. He seeks stability for his own domain, redirecting resources and influence to ensure his people endure — regardless of the cost to others.

The common good is being redefined as personal duty.  
Masaru must be stopped before self-preservation becomes doctrine.

**Masaru → Tank**

**Theme:** Protector who prioritizes his own domain

**Core Passive**

Masaru gains increased durability when positioned near a limited number of allies, but loses effectiveness when surrounded by many.

**Signature Action**

Raises a defensive stance that redirects enemy pressure toward himself while reinforcing nearby allies.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger protection for close allies
* Increased durability in small formations
* Better punishment for enemies focusing others

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Masaru **anchors small cores**, but discourages wide, cooperative play.

## Chapter III — Confrontation: Balance for the Few

With Masaru defeated, the Shogunate attempts to restore shared governance. Councils reconvene. Mediation resumes.

They are ignored.

A new figure gains influence: **Hiroto**.

Hiroto preaches balance not as fairness, but as efficiency. In his view, harmony is best preserved when those capable of maintaining it hold authority indefinitely. Equality, he argues, is instability.

Under his guidance, balance narrows. Many are excluded so that few may remain stable.

This is not peace.  
It is selective order.

Hiroto must be confronted before balance becomes privilege.

**Hiroto → Support**

**Theme:** Architect of selective balance

**Core Passive**

Hiroto periodically empowers the strongest ally on the board, increasing their effectiveness while neglecting weaker ones.

**Signature Action**

Issues a focused command that enhances a priority ally’s actions and suppresses nearby enemies.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster identification of priority allies
* Stronger empowerment effects
* Greater suppression of non-favored targets

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Hiroto **funnels power upward**, making strong units stronger at the cost of team equality.

## Chapter IV — Revelation: The Broken Scale

The defeat of Hiroto exposes a deeper failure. Balance has become justification rather than principle.

At the heart of this transformation is **Shiori**, a revered arbiter who has abandoned the idea of impartiality. She believes true balance can only be achieved by those willing to protect themselves first. Altruism, in her view, is weakness.

Decisions are no longer weighed evenly.  
They are weighted toward survival of the self.

If Shiori prevails, the Shogunate will endure — but only as a collection of protected individuals, not a unified people.

**Shiori → Caster**

**Theme:** Arbiter who abandons impartiality

**Core Passive**

Shiori applies weighted judgment to enemies, causing those who act aggressively or selfishly to suffer increasing penalties.

**Signature Action**

Releases a balancing wave that disrupts enemy actions and redistributes pressure unevenly across the battlefield.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Stronger judgment penalties
* Wider disruption effects
* Better control over enemy tempo

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Shiori **punishes excess**, but enforces balance selectively rather than fairly.

## Chapter V — Resolution: Harmony Abandoned

With Shiori removed, the Shogunate faces the consequences of prolonged imbalance. Trust is broken. Cooperation is fragile.

From this fracture rises **Renji**.

Renji does not reject peace. He rejects responsibility. He acts solely in his own interest, disengaging from all mediation while benefiting from the sacrifices of others. In doing so, he embodies the final corruption of balance: harmony without obligation.

Balance has been reduced to convenience.

Renji must be confronted as the endpoint of the Path of Balance — where peace survives only for those who take it.

**Renji → Assassin**

**Theme:** Harmony without obligation

**Core Passive**

Renji gains bonuses when attacking enemies already weakened or distracted by allies, thriving on others’ effort.

**Signature Action**

Strikes opportunistically, executing vulnerable targets before retreating from direct conflict.

**Enhancement Direction**

* Faster execution windows
* Stronger bonuses against distracted targets
* Better disengagement after attacks

**Gameplay takeaway:**  
Renji **profits from imbalance**, ending fights without contributing to their stability.

## Epilogue — Unlocked After Resolution

*Unlocked after completing Chapter V.*

With Renji defeated, the Shogunate does not immediately recover. Balance cannot be restored by decree. Trust must be rebuilt slowly, deliberately, and collectively.

Peace remains possible — but only if shared sacrifice returns to its foundation.

The Path of Balance endures, forever changed.
